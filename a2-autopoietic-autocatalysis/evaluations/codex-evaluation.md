# A² Proposal Cross-Evaluation

Baseline first: RAF closure is weaker than autopoiesis. RAF only requires internally catalyzed, food-generated reactions; autopoiesis additionally requires self-produced boundary and self-production of all constitutive components; Rosen goes further by demanding closure over the producers themselves. That matters here because all three proposals slide between those levels. See [02-autocatalytic-sets.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/research/02-autocatalytic-sets.md#L90), [02-autocatalytic-sets.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/research/02-autocatalytic-sets.md#L149), [01-autopoiesis.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/research/01-autopoiesis.md#L19), [01-autopoiesis.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/research/01-autopoiesis.md#L120), [04-self-producing-systems.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/research/04-self-producing-systems.md#L34).

## Claude

- **Theoretical grounding:** Best of the three. It explicitly distinguishes RAF, Rosen, and autopoiesis instead of collapsing them, and it uses organization/structure plus perturbation/structural coupling in ways that match the research unusually well [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L11), [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L242), [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L266). The weakness is that its “catalysis” definition gets loose fast: once any enabling artifact counts as a catalyst, the RAF mapping becomes a software analogy with a graph algorithm attached, not a rigorous transliteration of RAF.
- **Architectural concreteness:** Strong. This is a real architecture, not just theory cosplay. Seven components, a bootstrap, typed enzyme protocol, a RAF detection loop, fitness stack, and explicit failure modes are enough to start building a serious prototype [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L25), [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L394), [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L432).
- **Autocatalytic closure:** Partial, not genuine. It gets closer than the others by requiring the evolver, cortex, and immune system to become self-improving, but it still bottoms out in human-frozen invariants and human audit at higher levels [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L254), [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L300), [claude-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/claude-proposal.md#L521). So this is not full autopoiesis; it is a strong attempt at organizational self-production over an externally anchored substrate.
- **Novelty:** The `cortex` plus explicit maxRAF/irrRAF health metric is the most original contribution. Claude is the only one that really tries to make closure machine-checkable instead of rhetorical.
- **Weaknesses:** It overclaims the membrane. API policies and ACLs are not the same thing as a self-produced topological boundary. “Rebuild the whole genome from the food set” is underspecified under stochastic LLMs. DDC-style model cross-checking is useful, but it is not the same thing as true independent compilation or objective verification.

## Gemini

- **Theoretical grounding:** Weakest by a wide margin. It names RAF and Rosen, but mostly does symbolic relabeling: molecules become modules, catalysts become personas, and that is treated as theory [gemini-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/gemini-proposal.md#L12). It never really engages autopoiesis’ stricter requirements around boundary constitution and internal production of all components.
- **Architectural concreteness:** Thin. Rust, Bazel, MCP, Actix, and a vector knowledge graph are stack choices, not an architecture [gemini-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/gemini-proposal.md#L64). There is no detailed control plane, no protocol, no actual closure test, and no convincing account of how new catalysts are defined, validated, and promoted.
- **Autocatalytic closure:** No. The proposal quietly makes the “Primary Seed” part of the food set [gemini-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/gemini-proposal.md#L23), then declares autopoiesis achieved once the system can reproduce itself from that food set [gemini-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/gemini-proposal.md#L36). That is a definitional escape hatch. If the human seed remains in the food set, the system never actually closes over its own production.
- **Novelty:** The best idea here is the emphasis on **causal transparency** and the warning against becoming a thin allopoietic wrapper around vendor model priors [gemini-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/gemini-proposal.md#L75). That is a real concern the others should take more seriously.
- **Weaknesses:** It is hand-wavy almost everywhere that matters. “Stateful catalysts” are asserted, not specified. “Boundary = API surface + build graph” is not an autopoietic boundary. “Selection is additive and monotonic” is naive for a recursive self-modifier. This reads like a smart abstract, not a credible system design.

## Codex

- **Theoretical grounding:** Second-best in ambition, best in discipline. It uses RAF for construction, autopoiesis for identity, and von Neumann for germline/constructor duality, while explicitly admitting that software only gets a partial, organizational boundary [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L19), [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L145). It says less about Rosen than Claude, but what it says is more careful and less inflated.
- **Architectural concreteness:** Strongest overall. Workcells, germline, governor, archive, protocol objects, crate layout, Bazel targets, and promotion flow make this clearly buildable [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L33), [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L221), [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L276).
- **Autocatalytic closure:** Deliberately incomplete. The hard shell, hidden sentinel suite, trust root, and partially external judge mean this proposal does **not** achieve genuine autopoietic closure [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L135), [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L160), [codex-proposal.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/skunkworks/a2-autopoietic-autocatalysis/proposals/codex-proposal.md#L322). But unlike Gemini, it does not smuggle that fact. It names it as the central tradeoff.
- **Novelty:** The colony/workcell/germline framing is the cleanest software-native move in the set. The soma vs germline vs organizational fitness split is also the best treatment of recursive selection pressure.
- **Weaknesses:** Because the trusted judge remains outside, this is closer to an evolutionary software breeding system than an autopoietic unity. It is probably the most buildable architecture here, but it is also the farthest from the strongest biological reading of A². It also lacks Claude’s explicit formal closure machinery; the catalytic story is persuasive but not computably enforced.

## Synthesis

- **Where all three converge:** RAF is the right construction scaffold. Bootstrapping must start from a tiny seed and pass a self-hosting fixed-point test. The real bottleneck is not code generation but trustworthy evaluation of self-modification. Hermetic builds, rollback, lineage, and a slower-moving trust layer are non-optional.
- **Where they contradict:** The core design decision is whether the judge/membrane can become self-produced. Claude says “mostly yes, eventually.” Codex says “not safely; keep a hard shell.” Gemini gestures at “yes” but sneaks the human seed into the food set, so its answer is effectively “not really.” The other big split is organism vs colony: Claude imagines one reflective metabolism; Codex imagines a lineage of ephemeral descendants.
- **What none of them address:** None solve the fact that vendor LLM APIs remain external efficient causes. None define semantic equivalence for stochastic self-rebuilds. None really solve objective grounding: human goals, trust roots, and benchmark curation stay exogenous. None provide a substrate-level membrane stronger than policy files and ACLs.
- **Strongest combined architecture:** Take Codex’s lineage-governed workcell colony as the outer operating model. Add Claude’s typed artifact/enzyme protocol, maxRAF/irrRAF analysis, and cortex bottleneck detection as the internal self-model. Keep a minimal hard shell outside the mutable boundary: keys, substrate ACLs, hidden sentinels, rollback authority. Import Gemini’s strongest idea by making **capability internalization** a first-class objective: any recurrent behavior learned from external models must be progressively captured as code, tools, distilled local models, or testable policies. Then define explicit closure levels instead of pretending to have full autopoiesis: self-hosting, mutable-component RAF closure, repair-machinery closure, soft-membrane self-production, and reduced dependence on external efficient causes.

Bottom line: if the bar is **genuine autopoiesis**, none of the three get there. Claude is closest in theory, Codex is strongest in engineering, and Gemini is mostly rhetoric. If the bar is **credible autonomous software-factory architecture**, Codex wins, Claude supplies the best formal machinery to strengthen it, and Gemini contributes one important warning about causal transparency.
